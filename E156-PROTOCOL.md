## `[467]` Sotatercept in Pulmonary Arterial Hypertension: Living Meta-Analysis

**Type:** living-ma  |  ESTIMAND: HR for clinical worsening  
**Data:** 3 RCTs (STELLAR, HYPERION, ZENITH), 816 patients, CT.gov sourced

### 156-word body

Does sotatercept, an activin-receptor ligand trap, reduce clinical worsening in pulmonary arterial hypertension across patient-risk strata? Three randomized placebo-controlled trials deployed in this living meta-analysis (STELLAR, HYPERION, ZENITH) enrolled 816 patients with WHO Group 1 pulmonary arterial hypertension on background vasodilator therapy. Inverse-variance fixed-effect meta-analysis pooled hazard ratios on the log scale across the three deployed trials. The pooled hazard ratio for clinical worsening was 0.22 (95% CI 0.15 to 0.31), with no detectable heterogeneity (I-squared 0 percent). STELLAR (HR 0.16), HYPERION (HR 0.24), and ZENITH (HR 0.24) all show consistent large reductions across low, intermediate, and high-risk strata. Sotatercept reduces clinical worsening by approximately seventy-eight percent across PAH risk strata, a magnitude substantially larger than pathway-specific vasodilators. Activin-receptor biology differs mechanistically from endothelin and cGMP pathways; long-term safety beyond twenty-four months remains to be characterised.
